# Scope 3 Framework & PCAF
## Complete GHG Protocol Context for Financial Institutions

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** February 9, 2026  
**Purpose:** Clarify the relationship between all Scope 3 categories and PCAF's specific focus

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Key Points:**
1. **GHG Protocol defines 15 Scope 3 categories** for all companies
2. **PCAF focuses ONLY on Category 15 (Investments)** for financial institutions
3. **Category 15 is typically >90% of FI's total Scope 3 emissions**
4. **PCAF measures Scope 1+2+3 of investees/insured entities** (not FI's own emissions)

## 2. ALL 15 SCOPE 3 CATEGORIES (GHG PROTOCOL)

### UPSTREAM CATEGORIES (1-8)

#### **Category 1: Purchased Goods and Services**
- **What:** Extraction, production, transport of goods/services purchased
- **FI Examples:** IT equipment, office supplies, consulting services
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Low-Medium
- **Aviva Example:** Paper, computers, professional services

#### **Category 2: Capital Goods**
- **What:** Production of capital goods purchased/acquired
- **FI Examples:** Buildings, office fit-outs, data centers
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Low-Medium
- **Aviva Example:** Office renovations, IT infrastructure

#### **Category 3: Fuel and Energy-Related Activities**
- **What:** Upstream emissions from fuel/energy (not in Scope 1/2)
- **FI Examples:** Transmission losses, fuel production
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Low
- **Aviva Example:** Electricity transmission losses, fuel production for company cars

#### **Category 4: Upstream Transportation and Distribution**
- **What:** Transport of purchased products to company
- **FI Examples:** Delivery of office supplies, equipment
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Very Low
- **Aviva Example:** Courier services, equipment delivery

#### **Category 5: Waste Generated in Operations**
- **What:** Disposal and treatment of waste
- **FI Examples:** Office waste, paper recycling, e-waste
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Very Low
- **Aviva Example:** Office waste management

#### **Category 6: Business Travel**
- **What:** Employee travel for business purposes
- **FI Examples:** Flights, hotels, rental cars, trains
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Medium (higher for global FIs)
- **Aviva Example:** **REPORTED** - Air travel, hotel stays for business meetings

#### **Category 7: Employee Commuting**
- **What:** Employees traveling to/from work
- **FI Examples:** Personal cars, public transport, remote work
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Low-Medium
- **Aviva Example:** Employee commutes (sometimes estimated)

#### **Category 8: Upstream Leased Assets**
- **What:** Operation of leased assets not in Scope 1/2
- **FI Examples:** Leased office spaces (if not direct control)
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Low
- **Aviva Example:** Leased branch offices

---

### DOWNSTREAM CATEGORIES (9-15)

#### **Category 9: Downstream Transportation and Distribution**
- **What:** Transport of sold products to end customers
- **FI Examples:** Mail delivery of statements, contracts
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Very Low
- **Aviva Example:** Policy document delivery

#### **Category 10: Processing of Sold Products**
- **What:** Processing of intermediate products by third parties
- **FI Examples:** Generally N/A for financial services
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Not Applicable
- **Aviva Example:** N/A

#### **Category 11: Use of Sold Products**
- **What:** End-use of goods/services sold
- **FI Examples:** Generally N/A for financial services
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Not Applicable
- **Aviva Example:** N/A

#### **Category 12: End-of-Life Treatment of Sold Products**
- **What:** Disposal of products after use
- **FI Examples:** Generally N/A for financial services
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Not Applicable
- **Aviva Example:** N/A

#### **Category 13: Downstream Leased Assets**
- **What:** Operation of assets leased to others
- **FI Examples:** Properties leased to tenants
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Low-Medium (if FI owns real estate)
- **Aviva Example:** Leased real estate portfolio

#### **Category 14: Franchises**
- **What:** Operation of franchises
- **FI Examples:** Franchised branches (rare)
- **Typical FI Materiality:** Very Low / Not Applicable
- **Aviva Example:** N/A

#### **Category 15: Investments** ★★★ PCAF FOCUS ★★★
- **What:** Emissions from investments, loans, insurance portfolios
- **FI Examples:** 
  - **Part A:** Equity, bonds, loans (10 asset classes)
  - **Part B:** Capital market facilitation
  - **Part C:** Insurance underwriting
- **Typical FI Materiality:** **VERY HIGH (90-99% of Scope 3)**
- **Aviva Example:** 7.4 million tCO2e (700x larger than operational Scope 3)

## 4. THE TWO-LEVEL STRUCTURE

### Level 1: Financial Institution's Own Emissions

```
Financial Institution (e.g., Aviva)
│
├─ Scope 1: Company cars, heating (7,437 tCO2e)
├─ Scope 2: Electricity (413 tCO2e)
└─ Scope 3: 
    ├─ Cat 1-14: Operations (10,691 tCO2e)
    └─ Cat 15: Investments (7,400,000 tCO2e) ← PCAF PART A/C
```

### Level 2: Investee/Insured Entity Emissions

**PCAF measures the emissions of companies/assets in the portfolio:**

```
Category 15 (Investments) breaks down into:
│
├─ PCAF Part A: Financed Emissions
│   │
│   ├─ Listed Equity
│   │   └─ Investee Company A
│   │       ├─ Scope 1: Manufacturing (attributed to Aviva)
│   │       ├─ Scope 2: Facilities (attributed to Aviva)
│   │       └─ Scope 3: (optional, not yet reported by Aviva)
│   │
│   ├─ Corporate Bonds
│   │   └─ Investee Company B
│   │       ├─ Scope 1: (attributed)
│   │       └─ Scope 2: (attributed)
│   │
│   └─ ... 8 more asset classes
│
└─ PCAF Part C: Insurance-Associated Emissions
    │
    ├─ Commercial Lines
    │   └─ Insured Company X
    │       ├─ Scope 1: Operations (attributed to Aviva)
    │       ├─ Scope 2: Energy (attributed to Aviva)
    │       └─ Scope 3: Products (attributed to Aviva)
    │
    ├─ Motor Insurance
    │   └─ Insured Vehicle Fleet
    │       └─ Scope 1: Fuel combustion (attributed)
    │
    └─ ... 2 more insurance lines
```

**Key Point:** PCAF attributes a **portion** of investee/insured emissions to the FI based on:
- **Financed:** Outstanding amount / Enterprise value
- **Insurance:** Insured value / Total asset value (Insurance-to-Value method)

## 6. WHY PCAF ONLY FOCUSES ON CATEGORY 15

### 6.1 Materiality

**For Aviva (typical FI):**
```
Total Operational (Scope 1+2+3 Cat 1-14):     18,541 tCO2e (0.25%)
Financed Emissions (Scope 3 Cat 15):       7,400,000 tCO2e (99.75%)
```

**Category 15 is 399x larger than all operational emissions.**

### 6.2 Sphere of Influence

Financial institutions have **unique leverage** over Category 15:
- **Investment decisions** influence which companies receive capital
- **Lending terms** can incentivize emissions reductions
- **Underwriting standards** can exclude high-emission risks
- **Engagement** with portfolio companies drives change

### 6.3 Systemic Impact

- **Economy-wide effect:** FIs channel capital across all sectors
- **Transition enablement:** Green finance accelerates decarbonization
- **Risk management:** Climate risk in portfolios affects financial stability

### 6.4 Regulatory Focus

- **TCFD:** Requires financed emissions disclosure
- **EU Taxonomy:** Focuses on sustainable investments
- **Net Zero commitments:** Target portfolio emissions, not just operational

## 7. PCAF SCOPE COVERAGE DETAILS

### Part A: Financed Emissions

**What PCAF measures:**
- **Mandatory:** Investee Scope 1 + 2
- **Optional:** Investee Scope 3 (when methodology/data permits)

**Aviva's approach:**
- Reports Scope 1+2 ✓
- Plans to add Scope 3 "when methodology permits" ⚠

**Why Scope 3 matters for investees:**
- Some sectors have >80% emissions in Scope 3 (e.g., automotive, oil & gas)
- Full climate impact requires all scopes

### Part C: Insurance-Associated Emissions

**What PCAF measures:**
- **Insured entity's operational emissions:**
  - Scope 1: Direct operations
  - Scope 2: Energy use
  - Scope 3: Value chain (when material)

**Example - Commercial Property Insurance:**
```
Insured Building
├─ Scope 1: Natural gas heating (attributed to Aviva)
├─ Scope 2: Electricity (attributed to Aviva)
└─ Scope 3: Embodied carbon in materials (optional)
```

**Example - Motor Insurance:**
```
Insured Vehicle
├─ Scope 1: Fuel combustion (attributed to Aviva)
└─ Scope 3: Vehicle manufacturing (optional)
```

## 8. RELATIONSHIP TO GHG PROTOCOL

### Standard Hierarchy

```
GHG Protocol (Foundation)
│
├─ Corporate Standard (All companies)
│   ├─ Scope 1: Direct
│   ├─ Scope 2: Energy
│   └─ Scope 3: Value Chain (15 categories)
│       └─ Category 15: Investments
│
└─ PCAF Standard (Financial institutions only)
    ├─ Part A: Detailed methodology for Category 15 - Investments
    ├─ Part B: Detailed methodology for Category 15 - Facilitation
    └─ Part C: Detailed methodology for Category 15 - Insurance
```

**PCAF is a sector-specific elaboration of GHG Protocol Category 15.**

## 10. KEY TAKEAWAYS

### For Financial Institutions

1. **All FIs have Scope 3 Cat 15 emissions** (investments/insurance)
2. **Category 15 dwarfs all other emissions** (typically >95% of total)
3. **PCAF provides the methodology** for Category 15 only
4. **Categories 1-14 still matter** but are much smaller
5. **Two-level attribution:**
   - Level 1: FI reports Cat 15 as its own Scope 3
   - Level 2: Cat 15 consists of investee/insured Scope 1+2+3

## 12. GLOSSARY

**Scope 1:** Direct GHG emissions from owned/controlled sources

**Scope 2:** Indirect GHG emissions from purchased energy

**Scope 3:** All other indirect emissions in the value chain (15 categories)

**Category 15 (Investments):** Emissions from equity/debt investments, loans, insurance portfolios

**Financed Emissions:** Portion of investee Scope 1+2+3 attributed to lender/investor

**Insurance-Associated Emissions:** Portion of insured entity Scope 1+2+3 attributed to insurer

**Attribution Factor:** Proportion of investee/insured emissions assigned to FI based on financial stake

**PCAF:** Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials - sector-specific standard for Category 15
