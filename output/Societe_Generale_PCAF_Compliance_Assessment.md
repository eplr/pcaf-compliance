## Methodology notes

- **PCAF signatory**: Not confirmed in the report. SocGen uses PCAF Part A standard but no explicit PCAF membership statement found. They are founding NZBA members (L22519) and contribute to PACTA methodology development (L19119).
- **CRE caveat**: Commercial real estate is scored `reported` (1/1) based on NZBA physical intensity data (L19373, L22558), but SocGen explicitly excludes *«prêts immobiliers»* from PCAF Cat 15 (L20110). The CRE tracking uses NZBA/PACTA physical intensity (kg CO2e/m²) rather than PCAF loan/NAV attribution. Both approaches give 1/1 pt since the max is 1 (no S3 coefficient for CRE). If the grader considers NZBA-only CRE data insufficient for PCAF credit, score would drop to 14/18 = 77.8% (still Level 4).
- **Part B gap**: SocGen is a top global investment bank (DCM, ECM, syndicated loans). The absence of Part B PCAF disclosure represents a significant compliance gap for a bank of this size.
- **22% primary data**: This proxy for DQS corresponds to approximately DQS 3.8 using PCAF scoring conventions, which would score 2/5. However, since it is not explicitly labeled as PCAF DQS, V3 rule applies: Score 0.
- **Insurance subsidiary**: Société Générale Assurances has €146bn AuM (L19059) and is a Net Zero Asset Owner Alliance member. Its investment emissions are in Cat 15. Part C IAE is not reported.
