## Methodology notes

- **PCAF signatory + standard co-developer**: Swiss Re chaired the PCAF IAE working group (L1484) and co-developed the PCAF Part C IAE standard (L305–308). This is the strongest PCAF engagement found in this batch. Part C Attribution upgraded from 3/5 to **4/5** vs prior version.
- **Part A limitation**: Swiss Re’s primary investment GHG metric is WACI (revenue-based, L2099), not PCAF financed emissions. PCAF EVIC absolute financed emissions are disclosed as a supplementary metric only. This keeps Part A Attribution at 3/5 despite the PCAF leadership.
- **LULUCF exclusion**: Sovereign bonds explicitly exclude LULUCF (L2215) — consistent with PCAF data uncertainty rationale but limits sovereign debt to 0.5/2.0 pts.
- **IAE DQS (Part C)**: DQS 4.0 in 2024 (L3266–3271) is disclosed per PCAF standard — this belongs to Part C, not Part A. V3 rule: Part A DQS must be separately disclosed for the investment portfolio.
- **Treaty reinsurance gap**: Treaty reinsurance (74% of P&C GWP) has no IAE methodology yet. Swiss Re actively engaged with PCAF in 2024 to develop it (L1998). This is the main Part C gap.
- **Portfolio coverage (Part A)**: 67% confirmed at L1521. The 23% figure (L1993) is IAE CDP data quality (company-level reported data for Part C), not investment portfolio coverage.
