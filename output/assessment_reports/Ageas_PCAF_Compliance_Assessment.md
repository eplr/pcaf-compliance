## Methodology notes

- **PCAF methodology, not signatory**: Ageas uses PCAF methodology for both Part A (L3098, L5404) and Part C (L5395, L3080) but is not listed as a formal PCAF signatory. This limits Attribution Methodology to 3/5 for both parts.
- **Expanded scope in 2024**: Sovereign bonds and IAE were added in 2024 (L3270). The 2023 financed emissions figure (935,199 tCO2e) covers only equities+bonds, making year-on-year comparison impossible for Part A. The +457% increase (L3208) reflects scope expansion, not emissions growth.
- **Portfolio coverage explicitly disclosed**: 71% including infrastructure gaps (L3297). This is one of the few insurers with an explicit PCAF-compatible portfolio coverage percentage.
- **Personal motor DQS strong**: DQS 2.2 for UK/Portugal and 3.8 for Belgium (L5397). Vehicle-specific data with km driven. PCAF attribution factor 6.99%.
- **Commercial lines DQS weak**: DQS 5 (L5400) — based on sector median proxies due to lack of company-specific data from insured enterprises. Ageas acknowledges the challenge (L3083).
- **Reinsurance deferred**: L3085 explicitly notes *"La réassurance sera traitée dans une phase ultérieure."* This gap accounts for the difference between 6/8 (75%) and the maximum 8/8.
- **NZAOA member**: Since 2022 (L3093). 2030 target: −55% GHG intensity vs 2021 for equities+corporate bonds (L3096). Net-zero by 2050 (L3090).
