**Key observations:**
- Aareal presents a structurally atypical profile: ~70% of its credit portfolio is commercial real estate, a PCAF asset class with a maximum coefficient of only 1 out of 18. The same structural drag affects insurers on Part A.

- The largest scoring gap is the ~€5.8 bn in interbank loans (business loans class, 6 pts available), which are excluded from PCAF reporting without formal justification.

### Criterion 3 · Attribution Methodology — Score: **3/5**

**Level 3: PCAF-aligned methodology per covered asset class, but partial asset class coverage and a noted deviation**

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| CREF method (S1&S2) | Attribution factor = outstanding loan / property value; closely modelled on the PCAF standard but explicitly stated as not fully compliant with PCAF requirements "for technical reasons" |
| CREF method (S3) | Developed internally in 2025; applies a S3 emission factor (building type × country × floor area × market value) multiplied by the same S1&S2 attribution factor |
| Treasury – corporate bonds | EVIC as attribution denominator (PCAF-compliant); PCAF database emission factors (revenue- or size-based) |
| Treasury – sovereign debt | PPP-adjusted inflation-indexed GDP as both emission factor and attribution denominator (PCAF-compliant) |
| PCAF membership | Active member of the PCAF D/A/CH/LI regional network (CRE & Mortgages subgroup); published a dedicated PCAF Financed Emissions Report for FY2024 |

**Key observations:**
- The explicitly noted deviation from PCAF on the CREF attribution factor prevents a Level 4 score ("full PCAF methods, signatory").
- Active participation in the PCAF network and publication of a dedicated PCAF report signal a structured and committed approach.
- The S3 CREF method, while newly introduced in 2025, is a significant step forward not yet reflected in the dedicated PCAF report (which covers FY2024).

## 6. Strengths and Gaps

### Strengths
1. **Comprehensive CREF coverage**: 1,659 properties, €34 bn, near-complete S1&S2 coverage using PCAF database factors and EPCs as primary inputs
2. **S3 CREF reported for the first time in 2025**: an internally developed methodology covering downstream Scope 3 from financed commercial properties — a meaningful step beyond what most peers disclose
3. **Three years of financed emissions history** plus quantified 2030 and 2050 targets embedded in a formal transition plan
4. **Emission-weighted DQS** introduced in 2025, aligned with PCAF best practice
5. **Active PCAF network member** (D/A/CH/LI region, CRE & Mortgages subgroup) with a dedicated published PCAF report

### Main Gaps
1. **Missing asset class**: loans to financial institutions (~€5.8 bn) are excluded from PCAF reporting with no formal justification, representing up to 6 points of potential score on the raw coverage ratio
2. **Noted PCAF deviation**: the CREF attribution method is described as not fully compliant with PCAF requirements — a transparency risk and a barrier to achieving Level 4 on attribution methodology
3. **No LULUCF for sovereign debt**: 0.5 raw points not captured on the coverage ratio
4. **Part B = zero**: as a bank, the 30% Part B weighting structurally depresses the global score; this is inherent to the business model rather than a reporting gap
5. **Treasury S3 absent**: S3 for both corporate bonds and sovereign debt not reported, leaving 4 raw points uncaptured on the coverage ratio
6. **DQS 4 for Treasury**: corporate bond emissions are fully factor-based (no real data), limiting data quality improvement potential in the short term
