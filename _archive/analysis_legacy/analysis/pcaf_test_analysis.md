# PCAF Compliance Analysis: 3 Selected Reports

## PCAF Standard Categories (Reference)

| Part | Category | Definition | Asset Classes |
|------|----------|------------|---------------|
| **A** | Financed Emissions | Emissions attributable to loans and investments across 10 asset classes | Listed equity & corporate bonds, business loans & unlisted equity, project finance, commercial real estate, mortgages, motor vehicle loans, use of proceeds, securitized products, sovereign debt, sub-sovereign debt |
| **B** | Facilitated Emissions | Emissions attributable to capital market issuance activities | Capital market issuances |
| **C** | Insurance-Associated Emissions | Emissions attributable to re/insurance and underwriting activities | Commercial lines insurance, project insurance, personal motor lines, treaty reinsurance |

## Key Findings

1. **Santander** demonstrates the most comprehensive PCAF Part A implementation with emissions targets across 9+ sectors and explicit PCAF attribution methodology.

2. **Allianz** is the only entity reporting **Insurance-Associated Emissions (Part C)** per PCAF standard, and has robust financed emissions reporting for proprietary investments. Notably participates in PCAF methodology development.

3. **Amundi** reports operational GHG emissions but does **not explicitly apply PCAF methodology** to portfolio-level financed emissions. As an asset manager (not lender/insurer), their PCAF reporting obligations differ—they focus on ESG integration, Net Zero alignment, and carbon footprint metrics rather than attribution-based financed emissions.
